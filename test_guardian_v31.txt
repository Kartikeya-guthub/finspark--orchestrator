Project Guardian: Global Freelancer Payouts v3.1
Status: Draft for Review

Background:
Our platform helps Indian freelancers receive payments from international clients. Because we are moving money across borders, our compliance team is very strict. We need an automated engine to handle the KYC and Sanctions screening before a single dollar is moved.

1. The Identity Fork:
We have two types of users: Residents and Digital Nomads.

For Indian Residents, we must use the standard UIDAI Aadhaar v2.0 verification.

For International Users, Aadhaar won't work. In those cases, the system needs to trigger the Onfido v4.2 adapter for Global Passport/ID verification.

System Field: user_citizenship (e.g., "IN", "US", "UK").

2. The Watchlist Wall (Mandatory):
Before we do anything else, every single transaction-no exceptions-must be screened against global sanctions lists. Use the ComplyAdvantage adapter. We need to check the sender_name and receiver_name. If the API returns a "Hit" (meaning they are on a terrorist or money laundering list), the entire DAG must kill-switch immediately.

3. High-Value Gating (EDD):
We have a "Compliance Ceiling" of $10,000 USD.

If a transfer is under $10,000, just proceed to payout.

If a transfer is equal to or over $10,000, we require "Enhanced Due Diligence." The system must automatically trigger a Sahamati AA fetch to verify the source of funds before allowing the payout.

4. The Final Mile:
Once the identity is verified, the sanctions check is "Clear," and EDD is completed (if required), use Wise (Transferwise) for the currency conversion and payout. We'll need the beneficiary_iban and swift_code.

Data We Collect:
user_citizenship, resident_aadhaar, passport_photo_ref, sender_name, receiver_name, transfer_amount, currency, beneficiary_iban, swift_code.
